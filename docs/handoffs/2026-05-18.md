## Decisions made and why

1. **Two separate PRs (#65 code, #66 docs) rather than one combined PR.** Repo convention is "every PR branches from main, no stacking." Code review and docs review are independent concerns; bundling them creates a larger reviewer surface for no benefit. Trivial to land in either order.

2. **`since` becomes filter-only in `fetchPlaylistVideos`; no early-stop.** The reviewer's follow-up comment on #65 caught a real bug in the first patch: once filtering switches to `videoPublishedAt`, you can't early-stop on it because the uploads playlist is ordered by `snippet.publishedAt` (playlist-add time). A scheduled/private video added 18 months ago and made public last week sits on a later page with an old add-time but in-window publish-time ‚Äî the original early-stop silently dropped it.
   
   The reviewer offered two paths: (a) continue pagination, or (b) early-stop on `snippet.publishedAt`. Picked (a) ‚Äî strictly correct. Option (b) still has a false-negative for scheduled videos whose playlist-add time is itself > 12mo ago. For Simon's ~52-video channel the cost difference is zero (still ~3 quota units). For a misconfigured giant channel, `maxItems` is the safety net. Doc comments on both `fetchPlaylistVideos` and the `FetchPlaylistOptions.since` field call out the scheduled‚Üípublic case explicitly so a future "optimise this" pass doesn't reintroduce the bug.

3. **Full ¬ß13 rewrite + ¬ß18 item 6 update in one PR, not minimal-line patch.** Reviewer pointed only at ¬ß18 line 1241 ‚Äî but ¬ß13's process steps (1-6, RSS+GDELT historical labeling) were also stale and ADR-012 explicitly committed to updating them. Doing only ¬ß18 would have left ¬ß13 as a misleading literal-spec for any future reader. The SPEC.md size (75KB) made a `gh api PUT` with base64-encoded content the right transport ‚Äî recorded as a pattern for next time.
